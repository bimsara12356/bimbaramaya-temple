Welcome to Bimbarama Temple

A sacred place of worship and learning nestled in the tranquil village of Elukatiya, Ahangama.

About

Bimbarama Temple is a Theravāda Buddhist monastery that serves as a center for spiritual practice, meditation, and Dhamma education. It provides a peaceful environment for monastics and laypeople alike to cultivate virtue and mindfulness.

History

Established over a century ago, Bimbarama Temple has been a beacon of Buddhist learning in Southern Sri Lanka. Passed down through generations of learned monks, the temple has maintained its devotion to preserving the teachings of the Buddha.

Architecture

The temple features classic Theravāda architecture including a stupa, Bodhi tree, and shrine hall. Traditional murals and wooden carvings illustrate Jātaka stories, and a stone moonstone welcomes visitors at the entrance to the shrine room.

Monastic Community

The temple is home to a dedicated Sangha community who uphold the Vinaya, engage in Dhamma teaching, meditation retreats, and youth mentorship. Lay followers support daily activities and join in regular spiritual observances and almsgiving.

Rituals

Traditional chanting, offering ceremonies, and Poya day observances form the heart of spiritual life here. The temple also holds special rituals for Vesak, Poson, and other significant Buddhist holidays.

Theravāda Buddhist Events

  • Vesak: Celebrates the Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and parinibbāna. Lanterns, sermons, and Sil observance mark this day.
  • Poson: Commemorates the arrival of Arahant Mahinda and Buddhism in Sri Lanka. Celebrated with pilgrimages and Dhamma activities.
  • Blood Donation Drive: Community blood donation events are organized at the temple to support local hospitals.
  • Asalha Pūjā: Marks the Buddha's first sermon. Special sermons and discussions are held.
  • Vassāna: A three-month retreat for monks during the rainy season, with increased meditation and teachings.
  • Katina Ceremony: Held at the end of Vassāna – Lay devotees offer robes to monks as an act of merit.
  • Poya Observances: Monthly full-moon day observances with Sil programs, chanting, and Dhamma talks.
  • Sunday Dhamma School: Weekly classes for children and youth on Buddhist values, chanting, and ethics.

Location

Bimbarama Temple is located in the peaceful village of Elukatiya, Ahangama in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka.

Visitor Information

Visitors are welcome to attend ceremonies, meditation sessions, and Dhamma talks. Please dress modestly and respect temple customs. Alms giving is conducted daily at dawn.
